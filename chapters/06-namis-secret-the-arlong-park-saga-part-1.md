# Chapter 6: Nami's Secret - The Arlong Park Saga (Part 1)

- **Setting:** Conomi Islands, Cocoyama Village, East Blue
- **Key Characters:** Harry, Zoro, Usopp, Sanji, Nami, Arlong (Fishman), Nojiko, Genzo, Hachi, Bellemere (flashback), Captain Nezumi (corrupt Marine)
- **Scenes:**
  1. **Following Nami** — The crew sails toward the Conomi Islands in a borrowed boat (since Nami took the Merry). The mood is tense. Usopp is angry — she stole their ship. Zoro is suspicious. Sanji refuses to believe "Nami-san" could do anything wrong. Harry is quiet, staring ahead. He's not angry. He's worried. Something is wrong with Nami, and he's going to find out what. Let Harry talk to the crew about why they're going after her — is it for the ship, or for Nami herself?
  2. **The Conomi Islands** — They arrive at the islands and immediately sense something off. The villages are poor — run-down houses, thin people, no children playing in the streets. But there's a massive compound visible across the water: Arlong Park. A fortress built from stone, with a giant gate and a fishman flag flying. The villagers go quiet when anyone mentions Arlong. They pay a "tribute" — regular money to Arlong just to stay alive. Fishmen patrol the islands like they own them. Because they do.
  3. **Meeting Nojiko** — The crew encounters Nojiko — Nami's older sister. She has blue hair, a tangerine tattoo on her arm, and a tough exterior. She tells the crew to leave: "You don't know anything about Nami. You'll only make things worse." But Harry won't go. Nojiko sees something in his eyes and reluctantly agrees to tell them the truth. She takes them to a tangerine grove on a hill overlooking the sea. "This is where our story starts."
  4. **Bellemere's Story (Flashback Part 1)** — Flashback to years ago. A young Marine woman named Bellemere — tough, scarred, broke, with wild pink hair — finds two orphaned little girls in a warzone. Nami (a baby) and Nojiko (a toddler). She takes them home to Cocoyama Village. She becomes their mother. They're poor — Bellemere gives the girls all the food and goes hungry herself. She grows tangerines. She argues with Nami about wanting fancy clothes and maps. Normal family fights. Normal family love. Let Harry see what Nami's childhood was like — the happiness before it was taken away.
  5. **The Day Arlong Came (Flashback Part 2)** — Arlong and his fishman crew arrive at the Conomi Islands. Arlong is terrifying — tall, shark-like, with a saw-shaped nose, webbed hands, and teeth that grow back if broken. He's a fishman supremacist who believes humans are an inferior species. He declares a tax: 100,000 berries per adult, 50,000 per child. Anyone who can't pay dies. Genzo (the village sheriff, a man with a pinwheel on his hat) tries to fight. Arlong breaks him like a toy.
  6. **Bellemere's Choice (Flashback Part 3)** — Bellemere has 100,000 berries saved. Enough for two children OR one adult. Not enough for all three. She could lie — say she has no children, pay for herself, and the girls would be officially "unregistered." They'd survive. Nojiko and Nami hide under the house and beg her to pretend they don't exist. Bellemere looks at them. She can't do it. She can't deny they're her daughters. She pays for both girls. She has nothing left for herself. Arlong points his gun. Bellemere smiles at Nami and Nojiko: "Nami, Nojiko... I love you." Arlong shoots her. The girls watch. Nami is eight years old. Let Harry react to hearing this story. Let it hit him.
  7. **The Deal (Flashback Part 4)** — After Bellemere's death, Arlong discovers that little Nami is a genius at drawing maps and navigation. He takes her — drags her to Arlong Park and forces her to draw sea charts for him. She's eight. She draws maps in a tiny room, day and night, crying. Arlong makes her a deal: if Nami can gather 100 million berries, he'll free Cocoyama Village. Nami agrees. She becomes a thief — stealing from pirates, risking her life, for years. Every berry goes into her savings. She does terrible things. She hates herself. But she keeps going. For the village. For Bellemere. She's been carrying this weight since she was eight years old.
  8. **Nami in the Present** — Harry and the crew see Nami at Arlong Park. She's cold. She tells them to leave — "You're not my crew. I was never your friend. I'm Arlong's navigator. Go away." She says it with dead eyes. She's been saying lines like this for years to keep people from getting close. Sanji is heartbroken. Usopp is confused. Zoro watches quietly. Harry stares at Nami and doesn't believe a single word she's saying. Let Harry try to reach Nami. She pushes him away harder.
  9. **Zoro Gets Captured** — Zoro, scouting Arlong Park alone (and lost), gets captured by the fishmen. Arlong throws him into the underwater dungeon, tied to a pillar with a wound in his side. Nami finds him there. She secretly cuts his ropes and helps him escape underwater — she holds her breath and swims him out. Zoro sees it: Nami is still on their side. She's pretending to be the enemy to protect everyone. He says nothing. He files it away.
  10. **The Betrayal** — Nami is almost there — she's gathered nearly 100 million berries over years of thieving. She's so close to buying her village's freedom. Then Captain Nezumi — a corrupt Marine captain on Arlong's payroll — shows up with Marines and "confiscates" Nami's entire savings. Every single berry. Gone. Arlong arranged it. He never intended to honor the deal. He'll keep Nami drawing maps forever. The villagers watch from their windows. They've known about Nami's deal the whole time. They've been letting her fight alone for years, feeling guilty, waiting, hoping. Now it's over.
  11. **Nami Breaks** — Nami sits in the tangerine grove, alone. She has nothing. Eight years of suffering, stealing, hating herself — for nothing. Arlong will never let her go. She takes a knife and starts stabbing her own shoulder — the Arlong tattoo, the brand he put on her. Over and over. She's sobbing, stabbing, screaming "ARLONG!" — trying to cut him off of her skin, trying to cut him out of her life. Blood runs down her arm. This is rock bottom. This is a child who has been carrying the world alone for eight years and just watched it all crumble.
  12. **"Help Me"** — Harry appears behind her. He grabs her wrist — gently — stops the knife. Nami doesn't look at him. She's shaking. The crew stands behind Harry at a distance. Nami's voice cracks: "What do you know?! You don't know anything about me! Just go away! GET OUT OF HERE!" Harry is silent. Nami fights it. She doesn't want to need anyone. She's been alone so long. Then she breaks completely. She turns to Harry, tears and blood on her face, and whispers: "Harry... help me." Harry takes his straw hat — the most important thing in his world — and puts it on Nami's head. He turns toward Arlong Park. He starts walking. "Let's go." The crew falls in behind him. Four men walking toward a fortress. No plan. No strategy. Just rage and love. Let Harry feel this moment. This is why he's a captain.
- **Emotional Beat:** Bellemere's death — "Nami, Nojiko... I love you." Nami stabbing her tattoo. "Harry... help me." The walk toward Arlong Park. This chapter is setup — the payoff comes in Chapter 7.
- **Anime Moments:** Bellemere smiling before Arlong shoots her, Nami stabbing her own shoulder, Harry putting his hat on Nami and walking toward Arlong Park
- **Crew After Chapter:** Harry, Zoro, Usopp, Sanji (heading to fight Arlong)
