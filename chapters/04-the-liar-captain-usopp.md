# Chapter 4: The Liar - Captain Usopp

- **Setting:** Syrup Village, East Blue
- **Key Characters:** Harry, Zoro, Nami, Usopp, Kaya, Captain Kuro (Klahadore), Usopp Pirates (Pepper, Carrot, Onion), Django (hypnotist), Merry (butler)
- **Scenes:**
  1. **The Peaceful Village** — The crew arrives at Syrup Village — it's absurdly peaceful. Green hills, white fences, birds chirping. After all the fighting, this feels like a vacation. They need supplies and a proper ship. Nami heard there's a wealthy family here. As they walk into town, they hear screaming from the hill: "PIRATES ARE COMING! PIRATES ARE COMING! RUN FOR YOUR LIVES!" A long-nosed boy comes running down the road. The townspeople don't even look up. They wave and say "Good morning, Usopp." This happens every single day.
  2. **The Great Captain Usopp** — They meet Usopp properly. He's tall, lanky, long-nosed, and immediately claims to be the captain of a mighty pirate fleet of 8,000 men. His "crew" turns out to be three little kids with wooden swords (Pepper, Carrot, and Onion). Usopp tells elaborate lies — he fought a giant goldfish the size of an island, he once defeated a hundred pirates bare-handed. But he's also funny, creative, and clearly lonely. Harry mentions Shanks. Usopp's eyes go wide — his father, Yasopp, is on Shanks's crew. Usopp pretends not to care. He cares so much. Let Harry talk to Usopp about Yasopp — what does Usopp feel about the father who left?
  3. **Kaya's Window** — Usopp sneaks to the mansion on the hill every day to visit Kaya — a wealthy girl who's been bedridden with illness since her parents died. Usopp sits under her window and tells her wild adventure stories. She laughs, and for a few hours she forgets she's sick and alone. Klahadore, her strict butler, chases Usopp away every time, calling him "the son of a filthy pirate." Harry, watching from nearby, can see that Usopp's stories are the best medicine Kaya has. Let Harry meet Kaya and see the dynamic between her and Usopp.
  4. **The Moonlit Cliff** — Usopp is wandering the coast at night and overhears a secret meeting at the cliff. Klahadore is talking to Django, a goofy-looking hypnotist pirate. Klahadore reveals the truth: he's Captain Kuro — a genius pirate captain who faked his death three years ago. His plan: Django will hypnotize Kaya into writing a will leaving everything to Klahadore, then the Black Cat Pirates will attack the village and kill her. Three years of pretending to be a butler — all for her fortune. Usopp is horrified. He runs.
  5. **Nobody Believes the Liar** — Usopp runs through the village screaming "PIRATES ARE COMING! REAL PIRATES THIS TIME! CAPTAIN KURO IS ALIVE!" Nobody believes him. Of course they don't — he's cried wolf every day of his life. He runs to Kaya and tells her Klahadore is a pirate who wants to kill her. Kaya slaps him. She's angry — Klahadore has taken care of her. Usopp stands in the road alone, tears in his eyes. The boy who always lied is telling the truth for the first time, and no one will listen. Let Harry find Usopp in this moment. Harry believes him instantly.
  6. **Preparing for Battle** — It's the middle of the night. Usopp knows the pirates will come at dawn, up the slope from the beach. He decides to fight them alone to protect the village — even though he's terrified. His knees are literally shaking. His teeth chatter. But he goes to the slope and sets up traps: oil, caltrops, slingshot positions. Harry, Zoro, and Nami find him there. Harry: "We'll help." Usopp nearly cries from relief but tries to play it cool: "O-of course! I was just about to call my 8,000 men!" Let Harry help set the traps and plan the defense of the slope.
  7. **The Wrong Slope** — Dawn breaks. The Black Cat Pirates appear... at the OTHER slope. Usopp guessed wrong. Panic. They have to sprint across the entire island to the north slope. This is Usopp's nightmare — everything going wrong. But he runs anyway, screaming, slingshot in hand. Let Harry and the crew race to catch the pirates before they reach the village.
  8. **The Battle on the Slope** — The Black Cat Pirates charge up the slope — dozens of cat-themed pirates with clawed gloves. Zoro fights the Nyaban Brothers (Sham and Buchi) — acrobatic fighters who pretend to be weak, then strike hard. Nami guards the treasure (and herself). Usopp fights from the back with his slingshot — Lead Stars, Egg Stars, Tabasco Stars. He's scared out of his mind but he doesn't run. Every shot counts. Harry brawls through the pirates with Gum Gum Pistol and Gum Gum Whip. Let Harry fight alongside Usopp — encourage him, protect him, watch him be brave.
  9. **Django's Hypnosis** — Django the hypnotist tries to put everyone to sleep. He swings his ring: "One, two, DJANGO!" Harry falls asleep mid-punch (he's that simple). Usopp has to wake him up by hitting him with a slingshot pellet. Comedy in the middle of chaos. Django also accidentally hypnotizes his own crew into a berserker rage, making them stronger but dumber.
  10. **Captain Kuro Arrives** — Kuro finally shows himself. He's cold, calculating, and deadly. His weapon: long blades on each finger, like cat claws. He moves so fast he disappears — "Shakushi" (Death Scoop) — he slashes everything around him randomly, including his own crew. He doesn't care about anyone. He calls his pirates garbage. He calls Usopp garbage. He calls the entire village garbage. Harry is furious — this guy planned for three years to betray someone who trusted him.
  11. **Usopp's Courage** — Kuro threatens Kaya, who has arrived at the slope after learning the truth. Usopp stands between them, shaking from head to toe, blood running down his face from a wound. He can barely stand. But he plants his feet: "I'm a liar! I've always been a liar! But TODAY every word I'm saying is TRUE! I will protect this village!" He fires a Lead Star into Kuro's face. It barely scratches him. But it's the bravest thing Usopp has ever done.
  12. **Harry vs Kuro** — Harry fights Kuro. Kuro is fast, but Harry is rubber — slashes don't work well on him. Kuro's cat claws scrape across Harry's rubber body and stretch but don't cut deep. Harry catches Kuro's blades with his stretching arms and delivers a massive Gum Gum Bell (a rubber headbutt) that knocks Kuro out cold. The Black Cat Pirates flee. Syrup Village is saved. Let Harry say something to Kuro about betraying people who trust you.
  13. **The Going Merry** — Kaya, grateful and healthy for the first time in months, gives the crew a ship: the Going Merry. She's a caravel with a sheep figurehead, white sails, and a little cabin. She's not big, not fancy, but she's perfect. Merry (the lamb-haired butler, not the ship) designed her. Usopp stares at the ship and at Harry. Harry grins: "Join my crew. I need a sniper." Usopp tries to play it cool, fails completely, and says yes with tears streaming down his face. He says goodbye to the Usopp Pirates (the three kids). They salute their captain. Kaya waves from the dock.
  14. **First Day on the Merry** — The crew sails away on their first real ship. Nami takes the helm and actually knows what she's doing. Zoro naps on deck. Usopp is already sketching his pirate flag design (a skull with a slingshot and long nose). Harry sits on the Going Merry's figurehead — the sheep's head — legs dangling over the water, grinning at the horizon. This is what being a pirate feels like. Let Harry enjoy the moment. They need a cook next.
- **Emotional Beat:** Usopp's courage — "I'm a liar, but TODAY I'm telling the truth!" Nobody believing the boy who cried wolf. Kaya's gift of the Going Merry.
- **Anime Moments:** Usopp standing in the road alone after no one believes him, Usopp bleeding but standing in front of Kaya, the Going Merry sailing for the first time
- **Crew After Chapter:** Harry, Zoro, Nami (temporary), Usopp
- **Ship:** Going Merry
