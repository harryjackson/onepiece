# Chapter 14: Jaya - The Island of Mockery

- **Setting:** Jaya — a lawless pirate island
- **Key Characters:** Harry, crew, Bellamy (Spring-Spring Fruit), Cricket (descendant of Noland), Blackbeard (first appearance)
- **Key Events:**
  - The crew finds a galleon falling from the sky — proof that sky islands might exist. Everyone in town laughs at them.
  - Arrive at Jaya — meet Bellamy the Hyena, who mocks anyone who believes in dreams. He beats up Harry and the crew. Harry doesn't fight back — Bellamy isn't worth it (echoing Shanks's lesson about when to fight)
  - Meet Montblanc Cricket — a descendant of Noland the Liar. Noland claimed he found a city of gold 400 years ago but was executed when no one believed him. Cricket is searching the ocean floor to prove his ancestor wasn't a liar
  - Cricket helps the crew prepare to ride the Knock-Up Stream to the sky
  - Harry returns to Mock Town and defeats Bellamy with one punch
  - Brief encounter with Blackbeard (Marshall D. Teach) — he tells Harry: "A man's dream will never die!" Harry and Blackbeard agree on this without knowing they'll become enemies
  - The Knock-Up Stream launches the Going Merry into the sky
- **Scenes:**
  1. **A Ship Falls from the Sky** — The crew is sailing peacefully when a massive galleon crashes from the sky, nearly crushing the Going Merry. An ancient ship, covered in barnacles and sky creatures, falls from the clouds. Inside they find a map, strange shells called Dials, and evidence of a place above the clouds. Harry is immediately excited: "There's an island in the sky! Let's go!" Everyone else thinks he's lost his mind. Nami and Robin study the evidence. The Log Pose points upward.
  2. **Mock Town** — They arrive at Jaya — a lawless pirate island. Mock Town is rough, violent, and full of people who mock dreamers. Harry asks around about sky islands. Everyone laughs at him. "Sky islands? What are you, a kid?" The mockery is relentless. Let Harry feel what it is like to have his dream laughed at.
  3. **Bellamy the Hyena** — Bellamy is the big shot of Mock Town. Spring-Spring Fruit — his legs become springs, letting him bounce at high speed. He represents everything Harry is not: a pirate who thinks dreams are stupid. He beats Harry and Zoro bloody in a bar. Harry does not fight back. Zoro does not fight back. They sit there and take it. Nami is furious: "Why won't you fight?!" Harry: "It's not worth it." This is Shanks's lesson from Chapter 1 — some fights aren't worth having. Bellamy is not worth it.
  4. **Montblanc Cricket** — The crew finds Cricket — a diver living alone on a coast, searching the ocean floor for proof that his ancestor Noland was telling the truth. 400 years ago, Noland the Explorer found a city of gold on Jaya. When he brought the king to see it, the city was gone. Noland was executed as a liar. His family has been shamed for centuries. Cricket dives every day, risking his life, looking for gold on the sea floor. He has found some — just enough to prove it was real. Let Harry connect with Cricket's dedication to a dream everyone mocks.
  5. **Preparing for the Knock-Up Stream** — Cricket and his crew (the Saruyama Alliance — monkey-themed pirates) help prepare the Going Merry to ride the Knock-Up Stream, a massive undersea explosion that shoots water miles into the sky. It only happens at certain times in certain places. The preparation is frantic — reinforcing the ship, gathering supplies, timing the current.
  6. **One Punch** — Before leaving, Harry returns to Mock Town. Bellamy has stolen Cricket's gold — the gold Cricket spent years finding. Harry walks into the bar. Bellamy launches at him with his Spring-Spring powers, bouncing at full speed. Harry does not dodge. He punches once. One single Gum Gum Pistol. Bellamy craters into the floor, unconscious. Harry picks up Cricket's gold and walks out. No words. Let Harry deliver the punch.
  7. **Blackbeard** — Outside Mock Town, Harry bumps into a large man eating cherry pie. They argue — Harry says the pie is terrible, the man says it is delicious (or vice versa). They disagree on everything but then the man says: "A man's dream will never die!" Harry grins: "Yeah!" They part ways. This is Marshall D. Teach — Blackbeard. Harry's greatest future enemy. They agree on dreams without knowing they will try to destroy each other.
  8. **The Knock-Up Stream** — The moment arrives. The sea bulges. The Going Merry is positioned. A column of water EXPLODES from the ocean, shooting straight up into the sky at impossible speed. The ship rides the geyser up, up, up — past the clouds, past the atmosphere, into the white sea above. The crew screams — some in terror, some in joy. Harry stands on the prow, laughing, arms wide. They're flying. They're going to a sky island. Let Harry feel the rush.
- **Emotional Beat:** Harry not fighting Bellamy the first time (knowing when not to fight), then destroying him with one punch for Cricket's sake. Cricket's lifelong mission. Blackbeard's ominous first meeting.
- **Anime Moments:** Harry and Zoro sitting still while Bellamy beats them, Harry's one-punch knockout of Bellamy, the Going Merry riding the Knock-Up Stream into the sky
