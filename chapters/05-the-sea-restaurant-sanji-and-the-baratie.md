# Chapter 5: The Sea Restaurant - Sanji and the Baratie

- **Setting:** The Baratie (floating restaurant on the sea), East Blue
- **Key Characters:** Harry, Zoro, Nami, Usopp, Sanji, Zeff (Red-Leg), Don Krieg, Gin, Dracule Mihawk, Pearl, Patty, Carne
- **Scenes:**
  1. **A Restaurant on the Sea** — The crew spots something incredible: a ship shaped like a giant fish, floating in the middle of the ocean. It's the Baratie — a floating restaurant. Smoke rises from the kitchen. The smell of food carries on the wind. Harry is instantly starving. They dock and walk inside — it's a rowdy place full of rough sailors and amazing food. The cooks are all former pirates and thugs. The head chef is Zeff — an old man with a peg leg, a massive braided mustache, and a terrifying temper. Let Harry walk in and take in the chaos of the Baratie.
  2. **Harry Breaks Things** — Harry accidentally causes a disaster — he deflects a cannonball from a passing Marine ship (Lieutenant Fullbody's) and it destroys part of the Baratie's roof. Zeff is furious. He demands Harry work off the damage as a chore boy. Harry is stuck washing dishes, mopping floors, and bussing tables. He's terrible at it. Plates break. Soup spills. The cooks yell at him constantly. But Harry notices one cook who's different from the rest.
  3. **The Cook Who Never Refuses** — Sanji. Blond hair over one eye, sharp suit, cigarette (unlit for the kid-friendly version — he just holds it). Sanji is smooth, suave, and obsessed with food. He flirts with every woman who walks in (Nami gets the royal treatment; Zoro gets served scraps). But there's something else: a starving pirate named Gin collapses at the door. The other cooks refuse to serve him — he's a known pirate. Sanji cooks a full meal and brings it to Gin himself. No charge. Gin cries while eating. Sanji's rule: you never refuse a hungry person food. Ever. Let Harry witness this and react.
  4. **Gin's Warning** — Gin eats and recovers. He warns the Baratie: his captain, Don Krieg, is coming. Krieg is the strongest pirate in East Blue — he had a fleet of 50 ships and 5,000 men. But something destroyed them in the Grand Line. Something terrible. Gin is terrified just remembering. He leaves to report back to Krieg. The cooks are nervous. Sanji doesn't care — if Krieg is hungry, Sanji will feed him too. Let Harry ask Gin what destroyed the fleet. Gin can barely say the name.
  5. **Don Krieg Arrives** — A massive, wrecked galleon limps toward the Baratie. Don Krieg staggers in — covered in golden armor, bristling with weapons, but starving and broken. His entire fleet of 50 ships has been destroyed. Only his flagship survived, barely. He begs for food. The cooks refuse — he'll attack them the moment he's fed. Sanji brings him food anyway. Krieg eats, recovers his strength, and immediately betrays them. He pulls weapons and demands the Baratie as his new ship. Exactly as the cooks predicted. Sanji doesn't regret feeding him — "A hungry man is a hungry man."
  6. **The Hawk-Eyed Man** — What destroyed Krieg's fleet? The answer arrives. A small coffin-shaped boat approaches. On it sits one man: Dracule Mihawk, the Greatest Swordsman in the World. He wears a black hat, a cross-shaped sword on his back — Yoru, the strongest blade ever forged — and hawk-like golden eyes that see everything. He cut Krieg's 50 ships to pieces. Alone. For fun. He's bored. He's looking for someone worth fighting. The entire Baratie goes silent.
  7. **Zoro's Challenge** — Zoro sees Mihawk and his entire body tenses. This is it. This is why he trained. This is his dream — to defeat the greatest swordsman and take the title. Zoro draws all three swords and challenges Mihawk right there on the ocean. Harry tries to stop him — Mihawk is clearly on another level — but Zoro won't listen. This is what he lives for. Let Harry decide how to respond to Zoro stepping forward. Does he try to stop him? Support him? Watch?
  8. **The Tiny Knife** — Mihawk responds to Zoro's challenge by pulling out a tiny knife — a cross-shaped pendant no bigger than a dinner knife. "I don't hunt rabbits with a cannon." Zoro attacks with everything he has — Oni Giri, Tiger Trap, all his techniques. Mihawk blocks everything with the tiny knife. One hand. Barely moving. Zoro can't land a single real hit. The gap is enormous. Zoro is gasping, bleeding, and Mihawk hasn't even drawn his real sword. The horror sets in: this is what the top of the world looks like.
  9. **Zoro Faces the Blade** — Mihawk finally draws Yoru — the black blade, six feet long. He delivers one massive slash. Zoro could dodge. He could jump back. Instead, he faces it head-on, arms spread wide, swords at his sides. The blade cuts deep across his chest. Blood sprays. Zoro falls. Mihawk stares down at him: "Why won't you retreat?" Zoro, bleeding out on the deck: "If I take even one step back... I feel like I'd lose something important. A promise I can never take back." Mihawk is genuinely impressed. He sheathes Yoru: "Boy, tell me your name." "Roronoa Zoro." "I'll remember it. Surpass me, Roronoa Zoro. I'll be waiting."
  10. **Zoro's Vow** — Zoro, barely conscious, holds his sword up toward Harry on the Baratie deck. Blood pours from his chest wound. Tears stream down his face. He shouts with everything he has left: "HARRY! CAN YOU HEAR ME?! I'M SORRY FOR WORRYING YOU! I WILL NEVER LOSE AGAIN! UNTIL THE DAY I BEAT THAT MAN AND BECOME THE GREATEST SWORDSMAN IN THE WORLD... I WILL NEVER LOSE AGAIN! IS THAT OKAY WITH YOU?! ...KING OF THE PIRATES?!" Harry is crying too. Let Harry respond to Zoro's vow. This is the moment their bond becomes unbreakable.
  11. **Harry vs Don Krieg** — While Zoro recovers, Don Krieg makes his move on the Baratie. Krieg has hidden weapons everywhere in his golden armor — bombs, guns, poison gas, spears, a war shield. He's not strong because of skill; he's strong because he cheats, tricks, and never fights fair. Harry doesn't care about any of it. He fights Krieg head-on — Gum Gum Pistol, Gum Gum Gatling, stretching around the weapons. Krieg's armor shatters piece by piece. Harry finishes him with a Gum Gum Bazooka that sends Krieg crashing through his own ship. The Battle for the Baratie is over.
  12. **Sanji's Past — 85 Days on the Rock** — After the battle, as the crew rests, Sanji's backstory is revealed. When he was a child, Sanji was a kitchen boy on a cruise ship. A storm sank the ship. Sanji and Zeff washed up on a barren rock in the middle of the ocean. Zeff gave Sanji ALL the food — a small bag. Zeff kept a large bag for himself. They waited for rescue. 85 days passed. Sanji starved slowly, dreaming about the All Blue — a legendary sea where all the fish from every ocean gather in one place. When rescue finally came, Sanji discovered the truth: Zeff's large bag was full of treasure, not food. Zeff had eaten nothing for 85 days. He had eaten his own leg to survive. He gave all the food to a kid he barely knew. Sanji collapsed and swore: "I will never let a hungry person go without food. Ever. For the rest of my life."
  13. **Sanji's Goodbye** — Harry asks Sanji to join the crew. Sanji wants to — he dreams of the All Blue, and he can't find it at the Baratie. But he feels he owes Zeff everything. He can't leave. Zeff and the other cooks solve the problem: they start insulting Sanji's cooking. "Your soup tastes like dishwater!" "We'll be better off without you!" "Get out of here, you lousy cook!" It's all fake. They're giving him permission to go. Sanji sees through it immediately. He walks toward the door, then stops. He turns around, drops to his knees, and bows his head to the floor. Tears pour down his face: "Owner Zeff! Thank you for everything you've done for me! I owe you my life! I'll never forget your kindness for as long as I live!" The cooks are all crying too. Zeff, hiding his own tears: "Don't catch a cold out there, idiot." Let Harry watch this moment. Sanji joins the crew as the cook.
  14. **Nami Is Gone** — In the middle of the Baratie chaos, Nami stole the Going Merry and sailed away. She took the ship, the treasure, everything. The crew is stunned. Usopp is furious. Zoro is annoyed. But Harry looks at where the Merry was docked and says nothing for a long moment. Something is wrong with Nami. She didn't steal for greed — there's pain in what she did. Harry decides to go after her. Not to get the ship back. To find out what's wrong. They borrow a small boat and set course for the Conomi Islands.
- **Emotional Beat:** Zoro's vow to Harry — the promise to never lose again. Sanji's goodbye to Zeff — one of the most emotional moments in East Blue. Nami's sudden betrayal and the mystery of why.
- **Anime Moments:** Mihawk blocking Zoro with a tiny knife, Zoro's bloody vow to Harry, Sanji bowing on the floor thanking Zeff
- **Crew After Chapter:** Harry, Zoro, Usopp, Sanji (Nami has left)
