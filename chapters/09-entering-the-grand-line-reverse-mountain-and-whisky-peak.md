# Chapter 9: Entering the Grand Line - Reverse Mountain and Whisky Peak

- **Setting:** Twin Capes (base of Reverse Mountain), then Whisky Peak
- **Key Characters:** Harry, crew, Laboon (the whale), Crocus (lighthouse keeper), Mr. 9, Miss Wednesday (Vivi), Mr. 5, Miss Valentine, Igaram, Baroque Works agents
- **Scenes:**
  1. **The Whale at the Bottom** — The crew plunges down Reverse Mountain into the Grand Line — and immediately crashes into something enormous. A whale. Not just any whale — the biggest whale they've ever seen, blocking the entire canal exit. Its head is covered in scars from ramming into the Red Line. It swallows the Going Merry whole. Inside the whale's stomach, there's a small house, a lawn chair, and a tiny island — an old man named Crocus lives inside the whale. He's a doctor who keeps Laboon alive from the inside. Harry is furious at being swallowed. Let Harry react to being inside a whale's stomach — it's gross, weird, and kind of fascinating.
  2. **Laboon's Story** — Crocus tells the story. Laboon is an island whale from the West Blue. Fifty years ago, a pirate crew befriended baby Laboon and promised to return after sailing the Grand Line. They left him at the Twin Capes with Crocus. Fifty years. The crew never came back. But Laboon has waited, every single day, ramming his head against the Red Line — trying to break through to find them. His scars are from fifty years of headbutting a continent. He's been alone, crying, bleeding, waiting for friends who abandoned him. Let Harry hear this story and react.
  3. **Harry's Promise to Laboon** — Harry walks up to Laboon, who is bigger than a building. He punches the whale in the face. Laboon roars. Harry punches him again. They fight — a ridiculous, silly fight between a rubber boy and a mountain-sized whale. Then Harry stops, grabs a bucket of paint, and draws a terrible, ugly version of his Jolly Roger on Laboon's head. "There! Now we HAVE to come back — to fix this painting! It's a promise, Laboon. We'll come back. So stop hurting yourself." Laboon cries. Crocus smiles — first time in decades. This is a new promise to replace the broken one. Let Harry make the promise his own way.
  4. **The Log Pose** — Crocus explains how the Grand Line works. Normal compasses don't function — the magnetic fields are chaotic. Instead, navigators use a Log Pose — a wrist-mounted compass that locks onto the magnetic signature of the next island. Once it locks on, you follow it. Nami is fascinated. She's in navigator heaven. There are seven routes through the Grand Line, all converging at the end. The crew must choose a path. Two strangers — Mr. 9 and Miss Wednesday — beg for a ride to Whisky Peak. They're odd, secretive, and clearly hiding something. Harry agrees because he doesn't care.
  5. **Welcome to Whisky Peak** — The crew arrives at Whisky Peak and receives a hero's welcome. The entire town lines the streets, cheering, throwing confetti. Beautiful women fawn over Sanji. Enormous men offer Zoro drinking contests. Tables groan with food. Harry eats until he passes out. Usopp tells tales to adoring fans. Nami drinks everyone under the table. It's the greatest party ever. Too great. Suspiciously great. Let Harry enjoy the party while the sense of something being wrong builds.
  6. **Zoro Stays Awake** — Everyone passes out from food and drink — except Zoro. He sits on the rooftop, watching. When the town goes quiet, the "citizens" emerge from the shadows with weapons. They're all Baroque Works agents — bounty hunters who lure pirates to Whisky Peak with parties, then capture them for their bounties when they pass out. One hundred agents surround the buildings where the crew sleeps. Zoro smiles. He draws his swords. "One hundred, huh?" He fights all of them. Alone. In the moonlight. Without waking the others. Let Harry sleep through this and hear about it later — Zoro's casual dominance.
  7. **The Truth About Miss Wednesday** — After Zoro decimates the agents, the real threats arrive: Mr. 5 (Bomb-Bomb Fruit — can explode any part of his body) and Miss Valentine (Kilo-Kilo Fruit — can change her weight). They're here because Miss Wednesday's cover has been blown. Miss Wednesday is actually Nefertari Vivi — Princess of the desert kingdom of Alabasta. She's been working undercover inside Baroque Works for months, trying to discover the identity of the organization's leader — a shadowy figure known only as Mr. 0, who is engineering a civil war in her kingdom. Igaram — her bodyguard disguised as Mr. 8 — fights to protect her.
  8. **Vivi's Plea** — Vivi is desperate. Her country is being destroyed from within. A rebel army is rising against her father, the king, but the rebellion is being orchestrated by Baroque Works. Millions of people will die if the civil war breaks out. She needs to get home. She needs help. Harry, awake now and picking rice out of his hair, looks at Vivi and asks one question: "Which way is Alabasta?" Nami protests — Baroque Works is insanely dangerous. Vivi accidentally reveals that the leader is one of the Seven Warlords of the Sea. Nami: "We're all going to die." Harry: "Sounds fun. Let's go." Let Harry decide to help Vivi — what convinces him?
  9. **Igaram's Sacrifice** — Igaram disguises himself as Vivi and sets sail as a decoy to draw Baroque Works' attention. His ship explodes on the horizon — someone blew it up. The crew is horrified. A mysterious woman appears on the Going Merry — Miss All Sunday, Baroque Works' second-in-command. She's calm, beautiful, and terrifying. She gives them an Eternal Pose (a permanent compass to Alabasta) and tells them they can use it — or not. She's playing her own game. Harry takes the Eternal Pose and crushes it: "You don't get to decide our route." They'll get to Alabasta their own way. The crew sails into the Grand Line with a princess on board and a target on their backs.
- **Emotional Beat:** Laboon's fifty-year wait. Harry's promise to return. The crew agreeing to help a princess they just met, even knowing it means war with a Warlord.
- **Anime Moments:** Laboon ramming his scarred head against the Red Line, Harry painting his Jolly Roger on Laboon's head, Zoro fighting 100 agents alone in the moonlight
- **Crew After Chapter:** Harry, Zoro, Nami, Usopp, Sanji (+ Vivi as passenger)
